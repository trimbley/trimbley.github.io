What is a browser fingerprint?

A browser fingerprint is a profile built from properties your browser exposes to any website: the User-Agent string, installed fonts, screen resolution, timezone, language list, and how your device renders graphics via canvas and WebGL. Because the combination is often unique, a site can recognise you on later visits even if you block cookies or use private mode.

How canvas and WebGL fingerprinting work

When a page draws hidden text and shapes to a canvas and reads the pixels back, tiny differences in your GPU, graphics driver and font rendering produce a stable image hash that is specific to your machine. WebGL fingerprinting reads the unmasked GPU vendor and renderer the same way. These two signals are powerful because they survive most cookie clearing and persist across sessions.

Which signals reveal the most

Researchers measure identifying power in bits of entropy: more bits means fewer people share your value. Published studies put a full fingerprint around 18 bits, with fonts, the User-Agent, canvas and the WebGL renderer among the strongest contributors. FAQ

Is my data sent anywhere?

No. The scan runs entirely in your browser using standard web APIs; the values are displayed on the page and never uploaded.

What is a User-Agent string?

It is a line your browser sends with every request that names the browser, its version and the operating system. It is one of the oldest and most basic fingerprint signals.

Why does the canvas hash matter?

Drawing and reading back a canvas image exposes tiny GPU and font-rendering differences, producing a hash that is often unique to your device and stable across sessions.

Does clearing cookies hide my fingerprint?

No. A fingerprint is built from device and browser properties, not stored cookies, so it can persist after you clear cookies or use private mode.

How can I reduce my fingerprint?

Use a browser that normalises signals (such as Tor Browser or Brave), keep your browser updated, and avoid rare fonts, extensions and screen settings that make you stand out.

Are these values accurate?

They are read directly from your browser, so they reflect what any website can see. Some properties (deviceMemory, vendor, platform) are deprecated or approximated and may be missing.
